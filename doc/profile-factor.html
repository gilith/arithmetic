Profiling Factoring Algorithms

Randomly Generating Numbers to Factor

In the following |m| is the bitwidth of the natural number m, which is defined as |m| = if m = 0 then 0 else ⌊log2m⌋ + 1. The natural number n is always the product of distinct primes p and q such that |p| = ⌊|n| / 2⌋ and |q| = |n| - |p|. To generate a random natural number n with a desired bitwidth |n| we use the following procedure:

  1. Choose a prime p uniformly at random from natural numbers with bitwidth ⌊|n| / 2⌋.
  2. Choose a prime q uniformly at random from natural numbers with bitwidth |n| - ⌊|n| / 2⌋.
  3. If p and q are distinct and |pq| = |n| then finish, otherwise go back to Step 1.
